This workshop is part of the [National Fellowships in Fluid Dynamics Summer Programme 2024](https://nffdy8.wordpress.com/nffdy-summer-programme-2024/), funded by the UK EPSRC.

In this 1-day workshop, [Prof. Matthew Juniper](https://mpj1001.user.srcf.net/MJ_biography.html) and I have gone through the basic idea of how to infer parameters in a model by assimilating data in a Bayesian framework, and how to select the best model among many possible models based on the evidence (marginalized likelihood), i.e. by a type-II maximum likelihood optimisation. Then, we introduced [Bayesian-SINDy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.15357), a Bayesian recast of the classical SINDy algorithm. 

For the next half of the workshop, I continued with some tutorial on how to use [Turing.jl](https://turinglang.org) to infer parameters in a channel flow. 

Material for the tutorial can be found in the following link:

[Tutorial for Bayesian-SINDy](https://github.com/llfung/B-SINDy-NFFDy-Tutorial)

[Tutorial for Turing.jl](https://github.com/llfung/Turing-PoiseuilleFlow-NFFDy)
